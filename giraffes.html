GIRAFFES

A giraffe is a tall, long-necked mammal native to the African savannas and grasslands.

It is known for its distinctive appearance, including its long neck, long legs, and unique spotted coat pattern.

Giraffes are the tallest land animals, with adult males reaching heights of up to 18 feet (5.5 meters) and females reaching slightly shorter heights.

Giraffes are herbivores, primarily feeding on leaves from trees and shrubs, particularly acacia trees.

Their long necks allow them to reach high into the trees to access foliage that other herbivores cannot reach.

They are social animals that often gather in loose groups, although their social structure can vary depending on factors such as food availability and habitat.

Giraffes are also known for their distinctive behavior, including their slow and graceful gait, as well as their interactions with other members of their herd.
